Release notes

What changed in each version of Hindsight, newest first.

What's new in Hindsight, newest first. Hindsight keeps itself up to date, and you can check manually from the Colophon under The Apparatus → Check for updates.

1.1.0

Your Second Brain now understands you — not just the people, files, and projects in your work, but who you are and how your days have been going.

  • It knows you. Tell it your name and role (during setup, or anytime in Settings) and it speaks to you personally — greeting you by name and writing your day as someone it actually knows.
  • It learns you. From your own days it quietly works out things like your role, the way you work, and how you've been feeling — and asks you to confirm the important guesses before trusting them. Nothing is assumed, and anything that feels off is one tap to dismiss.
  • A new "You" tab. A home for your profile: a short portrait of who you are, the traits it has noticed, and a timeline of how your days have felt.
  • Private by default. All of this stays on your Mac and works with on-device AI. Sharing your profile with a cloud provider takes a separate, explicit opt-in.

1.0.12

A broad polish pass: more reliable meeting status, far wider accessibility, and a faster, more consistent interface.

  • Meeting status updates instantly. The menu-bar recording glyph, the permission prompt when a recording is blocked, and the "transcribing…" indicator now reflect what's happening the moment it changes.
  • Much better VoiceOver support. Buttons and controls across the app — Meetings, the Day Journal, the Second Brain, onboarding, and the Index — now announce meaningful names, and secondary text meets WCAG AA contrast.
  • Snappier reading. Opening a finished day, searching the Index, and reading streamed answers are all faster and smoother.
  • Clearer meetings. The section is consistently called "Meetings", larger calls get more distinct speaker colors, the connect-calendar prompt refreshes after you grant access, and a failed prompt now shows an error instead of doing nothing.
  • Rebindable meeting hotkey. The global start/stop shortcut (default ⌘⇧M) can now be changed in Settings → The Minutes.
  • Smaller fixes. Onboarding scrolls on small displays, the meeting audio player resets at the end of a clip, deleting a custom word asks first, and a failed app summary can be retried.

1.0.11

A fix so a finished day always shows up where you expect it.

  • New days appear right away. After you stop and save a day, it now shows up on the Days page immediately — previously a freshly finished day could stay hidden there until you quit and reopened the app.

1.0.10

Much better speaker detection, plus new ways to fix up a meeting after it's recorded.

  • Far better speaker separation. A one-on-one call is no longer split into a handful of phantom speakers, and genuine group meetings keep everyone — the speaker count is no longer capped, so a large call isn't truncated down to a few voices.
  • Re-analyze a meeting. A new button on a saved meeting re-runs transcription and speaker detection on the original audio — so you can pick up these speaker improvements on meetings recorded earlier, without recording again.
  • Rename a meeting. A pencil next to the title renames a meeting at any time.
  • Name a meeting from your calendar. One click names a meeting after the calendar event it overlaps, so your recordings match your schedule.
  • Refreshed meeting tabs. The Transcript / Results / Chat / Speakers tabs now use the same thumb-tab style as the rest of the app.

1.0.9

A formatting hotfix for AI answers.

  • AI answers are now formatted. Responses from Ask, the Second Brain, and meeting Q&A render their Markdown — bold, italic, headings, and bullet lists — instead of showing raw "**" and "*" characters.

1.0.8

Fixes meeting transcription.

  • Transcription no longer freezes. A scheduling deadlock could leave the live transcript blank during a meeting and the post-recording "transcribing…" step stuck indefinitely. Recording and transcription now run on separate lanes — the live transcript fills in during the meeting and the final transcript completes promptly.
  • More accurate speakers. Tightened speaker separation so a single remote participant is no longer occasionally split into two, and a slow speaker-detection step no longer holds up the final transcript.

1.0.7

Clearer day navigation.

  • Visible page controls. The Days page now has previous / next buttons and a dotted day indicator beneath it — one dot per day, with the current day highlighted. Click any dot to jump straight to that day. (The page-turn controls were previously invisible edge zones.)

1.0.6

Turning the pages — fixed, and nicer.

  • Day navigation works again. The left/right arrow keys (and ⌘[ / ⌘]) turn between days without having to click the page first, and the optional two-finger swipe (Colophon → "Turn pages with a two-finger swipe") is reliable.
  • A page-peel turn. Days now turn with a subtle page-peel animation instead of a flat slide. Honors Reduce Motion.
  • New app icon. A Daybook cover with the rubric-red ribbon bookmark.

1.0.5

Meeting recording now starts instantly.

  • No more waiting to record. Previously, starting a meeting could pause on "Preparing speech model…" while the on-device model loaded. Now recording begins immediately and captures audio from the first second; the speech model loads in the background.
  • Visible transcription progress. While the model loads, the transcript area shows a progress bar instead of a frozen screen, and the transcript fills in as soon as the model is ready. The full meeting is always transcribed when you stop.

1.0.4

A reliability release for meeting recording — especially your first one after installing.

  • Meetings wait for the speech model. If you start recording before the on-device speech model has finished its one-time setup, Hindsight now shows "Preparing speech model…" and begins once it's ready, instead of recording a meeting that comes back with an empty transcript.
  • A more responsive Mac while recording. Day Journal capture pauses for the duration of a meeting, so screen capture and live transcription no longer compete for the Neural Engine — and it resumes automatically when the meeting ends.

1.0.3

A small reliability release for first-run setup.

  • Clearer Screen Recording setup. When Hindsight is opened from outside your Applications folder — where macOS runs it from a temporary, locked-down copy — setup now tells you to move Hindsight to Applications and reopen it, which is the only way the Screen Recording permission will stick. Previously it pointed you at the "+" button, which can't take effect in that case.
  • Sturdier packaging. The disk image is now signed unambiguously, and the diagnostics behind the Screen Recording grant were tightened so first-run setup issues are easier to trace.

No feature changes — everything in 1.0.0 below is unchanged.

1.0.2

  • Fixed a launch crash on some Macs. The bundled Daybook fonts failed to load on Macs other than the one the app was built on, which could crash Hindsight at startup. Fonts now load reliably on every Mac.
  • Smoother Screen Recording grant. After you allow Screen Recording, setup now shows a clear Relaunch Hindsight step — macOS only applies this permission to a freshly launched app, so the relaunch is what makes the grant take effect.

1.0.1

A packaging release focused on a smoother, more reliable first launch.

  • Notarized and stapled. The app is signed, notarized by Apple, and the notarization ticket is stapled into the build. macOS can verify Hindsight offline, so the first launch is clean even without a network connection — no spurious "damaged" or "cannot be opened" warnings.
  • Steadier installs. Hardened the DMG so dragging Hindsight to Applications and opening it the first time just works.

No feature changes — everything in 1.0.0 below is unchanged.

1.0.0

The first release of Hindsight: a private, on-device daybook for macOS. One continuous book you turn through page by page, with three pillars and an Index (⌘K) that jumps anywhere.

Day Journal

A running record of your day, woven from periodic screen capture read with on-device OCR (Apple Vision). Turn between days with the left and right arrows, let the red Ribbon bring you back to today, and use Ask the book on any page to pose a question about your day or your history. Screenshots and the journal database never leave your Mac.

Meetings

Record and transcribe meetings on-device. Start one from the Record tile, the menu bar, the Index (Begin minutes), or the ⇧⌘M hotkey. Hindsight can watch your calendar and offer to begin a recording when a meeting starts, recognizing Google Meet, Zoom, and Microsoft Teams links.

  • Records your microphone and your Mac's system audio as separate, time-aligned tracks (the default; System Audio Only is also available).
  • A floating pill and a live panel with Notes, a speaker-labeled Transcript, and an Ask tab.
  • Saved meetings keep their transcript, notes, and any Ask conversation, and can be renamed and re-run through prompt templates.

Second Brain

A page that reads across every day you've captured, links the things that keep recurring, and forms plain-language beliefs about your focus. It moves between Chat, Insights, Network, and Export. The beliefs, Insights, and Network are computed locally and work with no AI provider connected; recall grounds every chat answer in your real history with citations back to the moment each claim came from.

On-device by default

  • Capture, transcription, and storage stay local. Meetings are transcribed on your Mac with the on-device speech model (Parakeet, which runs on the Neural Engine), and your journal database never leaves it.
  • No AI traffic without a provider you choose. Until you connect an AI provider, nothing is sent anywhere — Ask the book and the Second Brain simply wait.
  • A local provider keeps everything on your machine. Connect Ollama or LM Studio and your text is processed locally.
  • Cloud is opt-in. Sending anything to a cloud provider is off until you turn it on, and the same gate applies uniformly to journal text, meeting transcripts, chat, and drafts.

Requirements

  • macOS 14.2 or later.
  • Apple Silicon (M-series) — the on-device speech model runs on the Neural Engine.
  • A few gigabytes of free disk space for the speech model, which downloads once during first-time setup.
