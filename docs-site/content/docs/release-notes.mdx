What's new in Hindsight, newest first. Hindsight keeps itself up to date, and
you can check manually from the Colophon under **The Apparatus → Check for
updates**.

## 1.5.1

A dedicated embeddings model picker for Folder Context RAG — and three fixes the adversarial code review caught before they reached you.

- **Pick your embeddings model.** Folder Context RAG now has its own provider config in Settings → Intelligence → File Index, parallel to your chat AI. Point it at Harrier OSS v1 0.6B (or any embedding model) in your Ollama, LM Studio, or oMLX instance — independent of your chat model. A refresh-from-provider list helps you pick. With none set, files use the on-device model.
- **Saves take effect immediately.** Configuring the embeddings model used to require an app relaunch (a stale-capture bug froze the old config). Now the index and retrieval read the live setting on every call — change the model, hit re-index, and it's used right away.
- **File content never leaves your Mac.** The fallback path that used your chat AI for embeddings is now gated to local providers only — a cloud chat provider will never receive your file text for embedding.
- **Retrieval uses the right model.** A matching fix ensures the query vectors and the indexed vectors come from the same model, so semantic search actually works (it was silently mismatched before).

Requires macOS 14.2 or later, Apple Silicon. We recommend Harrier OSS v1 0.6B (the MLX 8-bit build) in your Ollama or LM Studio instance for file embeddings.

## 1.5.0

Your Second Brain now leads with genuine AI insight, reads your own files, and works reliably on local models — all on-device, all private.

- **The page now leads with what the AI actually did.** The Second Brain used to open with templated counting ("X sits at the center of a lot of what you do", cycling a few phrases). Now the AI Reflection — grounded observations written from your real captured moments — leads the page, and the templated belief cards are rephrased into varied, specific observations by the model. The vanity stat row is gone. With no AI connected, you get an honest "connect a model" prompt instead of fake templated confidence.
- **Your Second Brain can now read your files.** Point Hindsight at a workspace folder — a repo, a docs folder, a papers folder — and it indexes everything inside on your Mac: code, markdown, PDFs, images (via OCR), spreadsheets, rich text, email. The Reflection can then connect your screen activity to your own work artifacts, cited by file. "You switched to Slack right after the compile errors your plan flagged." Nothing leaves your Mac; embeddings run on your local model.
- **Local AI is now reliable enough to be the primary brain.** Hindsight now asks your local model (Ollama, LM Studio, oMLX) for structured JSON output instead of prompting and hoping — the change that makes a 3–8B local model dependable as your main Second Brain, not a fallback. Cloud stays optional and opt-in.
- **The Reflection checks itself, and sees the right frames.** Every reflection is now verified against your actual captured data before it sticks — a fabricated name paired with a real citation gets stripped. And it retrieves semantically-relevant frames (not just the most recent ones), so a reflection about "the migration" pulls migration moments from across the week.

Today's Focus is sharper and lands in Reminders:

- **Cleaner, more useful todos.** Citation markers and raw URLs/IPs no longer leak into your todo titles, and machine-internal detail text is replaced with human guidance. A tighter, higher-quality list.
- **Send to Reminders, not Notes.** The export now creates one checkable reminder per todo in a "Hindsight" list — native checklist, individually completable, syncs to your iPhone. Action items belong in Reminders.

Requires macOS 14.2 or later, Apple Silicon. Folder Context RAG and the local-AI improvements work best with a local embedding model (we recommend Harrier OSS v1 0.6B, the MLX 8-bit build, in your Ollama or LM Studio instance) — but everything degrades gracefully to the on-device model when none is running.

## 1.4.1

Today's Focus is now smarter, always visible, and reachable from every meeting.

- **Extract Todos from any meeting.** A new checklist button in the meeting detail reads the transcript and pulls out the action items — things you said you'd do, were asked to do, or agreed to. They land in Today's Focus immediately, each tagged with the meeting it came from.
- **Todos from real content, not just patterns.** When an AI provider is connected, Today's Focus now reads the actual text of your meeting transcripts and on-screen captures — not just the statistics — and extracts specific, actionable tasks ("Send the brief to Jane by Friday") instead of pattern inferences ("You haven't touched plan.md in 8 days").
- **Always-visible, always-refreshable.** The Today's Focus rail is now always on screen — even when empty, it shows what to do to surface tasks. A refresh button lets you regenerate on demand.
- **Auto-extract after meetings.** When a meeting finishes, its action items are extracted automatically — you don't have to do anything.

Requires macOS 14.2 or later, Apple Silicon.

## 1.4.0

Your Second Brain now genuinely thinks about your work — and the whole app got sharper, more honest, and more consistent.

- **The Reflection.** After you finish a day (or when you open Hindsight in the morning), your Second Brain reads the actual moments it captured — not just the counts, but what was on your screen, in what order, at what time — and writes a short Reflection: two or three genuine observations a thoughtful colleague might make. Every claim points back to the specific moments it noticed it in. It runs on your Mac if you use Apple Foundation Models or Ollama — free, private, no cloud needed.
- **A self-model that develops over time.** The Reflection's observations now become lasting beliefs. When the same pattern recurs across days, it firms up; when it doesn't, it fades. Recurring patterns promote to stable traits, and those traits shape what the Second Brain notices next — so its understanding deepens, not just accumulates.
- **The story of becoming.** The "Who you are" card on the You tab now tells a narrative — what the Second Brain has come to understand about you, how that's changed recently, and where it sees tension.
- **Today's Focus.** A new panel on Today surfaces suggested to-dos from your patterns — files you opened then dropped, topics that faded, action items from meetings. Check one off, dismiss it, or send the whole list to Apple Notes with one click.
- **Hide your voice from transcripts.** A one-click "Hide my voice" toggle on any meeting transcript. Your turns disappear from the view, but nothing is destroyed — bring them back anytime. It survives re-analysis too.

Trust and privacy hardening:

- **Every answer is now verified.** The Second Brain's honesty guard — which strips any claim it can't ground in your actual captured data — now runs on every chat answer, not just the relational ones.
- **Settings you can trust.** Three toggles that promised behavior but never actually did anything have been fixed or removed. The cloud-consent controls are now one clear decision instead of three cross-referencing toggles.

Plus: the Apple Notes Automation entitlement for one-click todo export, LLM-assisted phrasing of suggested to-dos when a provider is connected, meeting action items flowing into the Today's Focus rail, and dozens of accessibility improvements.

Requires macOS 14.2 or later, Apple Silicon.

## 1.3.1

A smarter privacy boundary for on-device AI — and a more consistent feel throughout.

- **Local AI now grounds on the real thing.** When you use an on-device provider (Ollama, Apple Foundation Models, or LM Studio), Hindsight no longer strips contact details from what the model sees — names, emails, and phone numbers are preserved so your Second Brain and chat answers reference real people and projects instead of `[REDACTED:name]`. Nothing leaves your Mac, so there's no privacy cost. Secrets (cards, keys, tokens) are still scrubbed for every provider.
- **Meeting chat speaks up when it fails.** A question to a meeting that can't reach the AI provider now shows the reason inline, instead of going silent or leaving a placeholder message behind.
- **One Send, everywhere.** The ask boxes in the meeting panel, meeting detail, and journal chats now share the same look and the same Send control — a consistent composer across the whole app.
- **Keyboard tabs in meetings.** Switch a meeting's Transcript / Results / Chat / Speakers with `⌘1`–`⌘4`, matching the rest of the app.

Plus the usual polish: standardized section headings and spacing, a centered empty state on Meetings, and sharper accessibility labels.

## 1.3.0

Find any day in your history at a glance, and never lose one to an accidental delete.

- **The Day Index.** Press `⌘L` anywhere to open a chronological overview of every captured day — title, date, and a thumbnail for each, with a density strip showing how much history you've built. Use `↑↓` to move and `Return` to turn straight to a day.
- **Recoverable deletions.** Deleting a day now removes it from the book but offers an *Undo* right after, from Today — so a mistake never costs you a captured day.
- **Always-on sidebar.** The book's Parts are visible by default on launch; unpin from the masthead toggle if you'd rather have the room.
- **Richer timeline.** Every activity row now shows a thumbnail you can click to open full-size — not just the starred moments.

A real privacy hardening for AI vision:

- **Secret-bearing frames never leave your Mac.** If a screenshot's captured text revealed a card, key, or token, its pixels are no longer sent to a vision provider — the text redactor can't see into the image, so the whole frame is withheld.
- **Honest about what vision sees.** The vision setting and the on-day redaction badge now say plainly: captured text is scrubbed, but visible pixels sent for descriptions are not.

And a sweep of reliability and polish: the recording control no longer lets *Discard* look like the main action; a failed Second Brain question now tells you instead of hanging; the "relaunch to apply Screen Recording" state stays visible until you act on it; a new multi-step progress bar in setup; and consistent ask fields, status dots, and delete confirmations throughout.

## 1.2.0

The Second Brain now answers from what your screens actually showed — not just who and
what kept recurring.

- **Answers from real content.** Ask "why do I keep working with this client?" and the chat reads
  the actual text of the relevant moments — emails, docs, messages — and grounds its answer
  in them, with citations you can click to jump straight to the frame.
- **It knows you're not your own coworker.** The Second Brain no longer lists you among the
  people you work with.
- **Tighter privacy at the edge.** When a cloud model is in use, email addresses and phone
  numbers are scrubbed from what's sent — on top of the existing secret redaction.

The Days page is also redesigned:

- **A sidebar for the book.** An always-available table of contents. It auto-hides to give
  the page room — hover the left edge to peek it open — or pin it from the toggle in the top-left.
- **Timeline previews.** Each moment in a day's timeline now shows a thumbnail you can click
  to open full-size.
- A bigger delete control, and clearer labels distinguishing asking the whole book from
  chatting with a single day.

## 1.1.1

Two fixes to the Second Brain.

- **Dismiss sticks.** When you dismiss something the Second Brain inferred about you, it
  no longer quietly comes back the next day.
- **Sharper recall.** Asking about a specific person or file now surfaces the right ones
  to jump to, instead of coming up empty on a normally-worded question.

## 1.1.0

Your Second Brain now understands *you* — not just the people, files, and projects in
your work, but who you are and how your days have been going.

- **It knows you.** Tell it your name and role (during setup, or anytime in Settings) and
  it speaks to you personally — greeting you by name and writing your day as someone it
  actually knows.
- **It learns you.** From your own days it quietly works out things like your role, the
  way you work, and how you've been feeling — and asks you to confirm the important guesses
  before trusting them. Nothing is assumed, and anything that feels off is one tap to dismiss.
- **A new "You" tab.** A home for your profile: a short portrait of who you are, the traits
  it has noticed, and a timeline of how your days have felt.
- **Private by default.** All of this stays on your Mac and works with on-device AI. Sharing
  your profile with a cloud provider takes a separate, explicit opt-in.

## 1.0.10

Much better speaker detection, plus new ways to fix up a meeting after it's
recorded.

- **Far better speaker separation.** A one-on-one call is no longer split into a
  handful of phantom speakers, and genuine group meetings keep everyone — the
  speaker count is no longer capped, so a large call isn't truncated down to a
  few voices.
- **Re-analyze a meeting.** A new button on a saved meeting re-runs transcription
  and speaker detection on the original audio — so you can pick up these speaker
  improvements on meetings recorded earlier, without recording again.
- **Rename a meeting.** A pencil next to the title renames a meeting at any time.
- **Name a meeting from your calendar.** One click names a meeting after the
  calendar event it overlaps, so your recordings match your schedule.
- **Refreshed meeting tabs.** The Transcript / Results / Chat / Speakers tabs now
  use the same thumb-tab style as the rest of the app.

## 1.0.9

A formatting hotfix for AI answers.

- **AI answers are now formatted.** Responses from Ask, the Second Brain, and
  meeting Q&A render their Markdown — bold, italic, headings, and bullet lists —
  instead of showing raw "**" and "*" characters.

## 1.0.8

Fixes meeting transcription.

- **Transcription no longer freezes.** A scheduling deadlock could leave the live
  transcript blank during a meeting and the post-recording "transcribing…" step
  stuck indefinitely. Recording and transcription now run on separate lanes — the
  live transcript fills in during the meeting and the final transcript completes
  promptly.
- **More accurate speakers.** Tightened speaker separation so a single remote
  participant is no longer occasionally split into two, and a slow
  speaker-detection step no longer holds up the final transcript.

## 1.0.7

Clearer day navigation.

- **Visible page controls.** The Days page now has previous / next buttons and a
  dotted day indicator beneath it — one dot per day, with the current day
  highlighted. Click any dot to jump straight to that day. (The page-turn
  controls were previously invisible edge zones.)

## 1.0.6

Turning the pages — fixed, and nicer.

- **Day navigation works again.** The left/right arrow keys (and ⌘[ / ⌘]) turn
  between days without having to click the page first, and the optional
  two-finger swipe (Colophon → "Turn pages with a two-finger swipe") is reliable.
- **A page-peel turn.** Days now turn with a subtle page-peel animation instead

- **New app icon.** A Daybook cover with the rubric-red ribbon bookmark.

## 1.0.5

Meeting recording now starts instantly.

- **No more waiting to record.** Previously, starting a meeting could pause on
  "Preparing speech model…" while the on-device model loaded. Now recording
  begins immediately and captures audio from the first second; the speech model
  loads in the background.
- **Visible transcription progress.** While the model loads, the transcript area
  shows a progress bar instead of a frozen screen, and the transcript fills in as
  soon as the model is ready. The full meeting is always transcribed when you
  stop.

## 1.0.4

A reliability release for meeting recording — especially your first one after

- **Meetings wait for the speech model.** If you start recording before the

- **A more responsive Mac while recording.** Day Journal capture pauses for the

## 1.0.0

The first release of Hindsight: a private, on-device daybook for macOS. One
continuous book you turn through page by page, with three pillars and an Index
(<kbd>⌘K</kbd>) that jumps anywhere.

### Day Journal

A running record of your day, woven from periodic screen capture read with
on-device OCR (Apple Vision). Turn between days with the left and right arrows,
let the red Ribbon bring you back to today, and use **Ask the book** on any page
to pose a question about your day or your history. Screenshots and the journal
database never leave your Mac.

### Meetings

Record and transcribe meetings on-device. Start one from the Record tile, the
menu bar, the Index (**Begin minutes**), or the <kbd>⇧⌘M</kbd> hotkey. Hindsight
can watch your calendar and offer to begin a recording when a meeting starts,
recognizing Google Meet, Zoom, and Microsoft Teams links.

- Records your **microphone** and your Mac's **system audio** as separate,
  time-aligned tracks (the default; **System Audio Only** is also available).
- A floating pill and a live panel with **Notes**, a speaker-labeled
  **Transcript**, and an **Ask** tab.
- Saved meetings keep their transcript, notes, and any Ask conversation, and can
  be renamed and re-run through prompt templates.

### Second Brain

A page that reads across every day you've captured, links the things that keep
recurring, and forms plain-language **beliefs** about your focus. It moves
between **Chat**, **Insights**, **Network**, and **Export**. The beliefs,
Insights, and Network are computed locally and work with no AI provider
connected; recall grounds every chat answer in your real history with citations
back to the moment each claim came from.

### On-device by default

- **Capture, transcription, and storage stay local.** Meetings are transcribed
  on your Mac with the on-device speech model (Parakeet, which runs on the Neural
  Engine), and your journal database never leaves it.
- **No AI traffic without a provider you choose.** Until you connect an AI
  provider, nothing is sent anywhere — Ask the book and the Second Brain simply
  wait.
- **A local provider keeps everything on your machine.** Connect Ollama or
  LM Studio and your text is processed locally.
- **Cloud is opt-in.** Sending anything to a cloud provider is off until you
  turn it on, and the same gate applies uniformly to journal text, meeting
  transcripts, chat, and drafts.

### Requirements

- macOS 14.2 or later.
- Apple Silicon (M-series) — the on-device speech model runs on the Neural
  Engine.
- A few gigabytes of free disk space for the speech model, which downloads once
  during first-time setup.
