---
title: Day Journal
description: Quiet on-device screenshots, on-device text recognition, an AI write-up of your day, and an end-of-day review — all kept on your Mac.
---

The Day Journal is today's page in your book. While it runs, Hindsight quietly
takes a screenshot of your front window every couple of minutes, reads the text
on screen so you can recall anything you saw, and — when you stop for the day —
writes the day up for you to review and keep.

## Start capturing

Open the Day Journal control on today's page and press **Start**. The first time,
macOS needs the **Screen Recording** permission; grant it under
**System Settings → Privacy & Security → Screen Recording**, then start again.

While capture is running you'll see:

- A **RECORDING** indicator with an elapsed clock.
- A running **FRAMES** count (screenshots taken so far).
- A **MOMENTS** count. Press **Mark Moment** to flag something worth
  remembering as it happens; you can add a short note to a moment, too.

To wrap up, press **Stop & Review**. **Discard** throws the session away.

## What gets captured

- **Screenshots.** A frame of your front window, taken on-device at your chosen
  cadence. The default is **every 2 minutes** (you can pick 30 seconds, 1, 2, 5,
  or 10 minutes).
- **On-screen text (OCR).** Each frame is read on-device so its text is
  searchable and recallable. Recognition runs entirely on your Mac.
- **Context.** The app and window in front, so activities are easy to make sense
  of later.

<Callout title="Deep read (off by default)">
  **Capture focused text (deep read)** reads the focused window's text through
  Accessibility for richer recall. It's off by default, stays on-device, and
  runs through the same redaction gate as everything else. Turn it on under
  **Settings → Capture** if you want it.
</Callout>

## The AI day narrative

As the day goes on, Hindsight periodically reviews the new screenshots and keeps
a running summary of what you've been working on. The default is **every 30
minutes** (you can choose 15, 30, or 60). When you stop, that work becomes the
day's narrative — a short, readable write-up that sits on the finished day's
page alongside a timeline of your activities, the apps you used, your marked
moments, and any notes you add.

<Callout>
  The narrative is the one part that draws on an AI provider. Connect one under
  **Settings → AI Provider** — a local provider keeps everything on your Mac.
  Capture, text recognition, search, and storage never need a provider.
</Callout>

## The end-of-day review

After **Stop & Review**, Hindsight writes up your day and then opens a short
**review** at the foot of today's page. It's a chat: it shows the running
summary, may ask a few follow-up questions to fill in gaps, and lets you add
notes. When you're happy, keep the day; or discard it. A kept day is saved as a
finished page you can turn back to anytime.

Any finished day also has **Chat with this day** — a free-form conversation
about that single day, answered from what was captured.

## Pausing capture

Press **Pause** during a session to stop new screenshots for a while. You can
note why with a quick reason — **Meeting**, **Break**, **Lunch**, **Personal** —
or type your own. Press **Resume** to start capturing again. The pause (and its
reason) is recorded, so a finished day can show when and why capture was
interrupted.

Hindsight can also pause on its own:

- **Pause when idle** stops capturing when you step away. On by default, after
  **2 minutes** idle (you can choose 30 seconds, 1, or 2 minutes).
- **Reduce work on battery** lightens capture and analysis on battery power.

Both live under **Settings → Capture**.

## Keeping and clearing screenshots

Screenshots are pruned automatically by the **Keep screenshots for** setting —
**30 days** by default, or 7, 90, or Forever. Lowering it deletes older captures
on its next pass; "Forever" never auto-deletes.

## Today's Focus

On the right side of today's page, **Today's Focus** surfaces a few suggested
to-dos drawn from your patterns — no AI provider needed:

- **Open loops** — files or topics you touched then dropped ("Close `plan.md` —
  you touched it 8 days ago and haven't been back").
- **Cooling threads** — things that went quiet ("Decide if the roadmap is still
  relevant").
- **Meeting action items** — bullets extracted from your meeting transcripts'
  "Action Items" sections.

You can **check one off**, **dismiss** it, or **Add to Notes** — which creates a
checklist note in Apple Notes with one click. (The first time, macOS asks for
Automation permission to control Notes; grant it once and it's automatic
afterwards.)

When an AI provider is connected, the to-do titles are optionally rephrased into
clearer imperative voice ("Finish and close `plan.md`") — but the detection
itself is deterministic and always works, even offline.

Today's Focus refreshes when you open the page and after you finish a day's
review.

## The Day Index

Press <kbd>⌘L</kbd> anywhere to open the **Day Index** — a chronological overview
of every captured day, newest first. Each row shows the day's title, date, frame
count, and a thumbnail of its most recent capture. Use <kbd>↑</kbd>/<kbd>↓</kbd>
to move and <kbd>Return</kbd> to turn straight to a day. A density strip at the
top shows how many days you've captured and the span of your history.

## Turning through your days

Use the left and right arrows to turn between days. The red Ribbon always brings
you back to today, and **Turn to today** is also available from the Index
(<kbd>⌘K</kbd>, shortcut <kbd>T</kbd>).

## Where your data is stored

Everything stays on this Mac, under your Library:

- **Screenshots:** `~/Library/Application Support/HindsightNext/journal`
- **Journal database** (text, timeline, narratives, notes):
  `~/Library/Application Support/HindsightNext/hindsight.db`

Capture and text recognition happen on-device, and nothing leaves your Mac
unless you choose to connect an AI provider for the narrative or for chat.
