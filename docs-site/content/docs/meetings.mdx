title: Meetings
description: Record and transcribe meetings on-device, with a live panel, notes, and an AI Ask tab.

Meetings is where Hindsight records, transcribes, and summarizes your
conversations. Recording and transcription happen on-device. Each finished
meeting is saved alongside your days so you can revisit it later.

## Start a recording

There are three ways to begin:

- **Record tile** — open the **Meetings** page (or the daybook's Today page)
  and click **Record** on the "Record a meeting" tile.
- **Hotkey** — press <kbd>⇧⌘M</kbd> from anywhere. The same shortcut stops the
  recording when you're done.
- **Menu bar** — click the Hindsight status item and choose **Start Recording**.

You can also begin from the Index: <kbd>⌘K</kbd> → **Begin minutes**.

When you start, Hindsight checks for Microphone and Screen Recording access. If
either is missing, it points you to System Settings. The first time, grant both
so capture can run.

## Microphone and system audio

By default Hindsight records two streams at once — your **microphone** and your
Mac's **system audio** (the other people on the call) — and keeps them as
separate, time-aligned tracks.

Choose the default for new recordings under **Settings → Audio sources**:

- **Microphone + System Audio** (default) — records your voice and the call
  together.
- **System Audio Only** — captures just what's playing on your Mac, with no
  microphone. Use this for recordings where your own voice isn't needed.

The microphone **Mute** control is available only in Microphone + System Audio
mode.

## The live panel

Click the floating pill to open the meeting panel. The header shows whether
you're **Recording** or **Paused**, the elapsed time, and live level meters for
the microphone and system audio.

From the header you can:

- **Mute / Unmute** the microphone (mic + system mode only). Muting also pauses
  live transcription of your voice.
- **Pause / Resume** — pausing stops capture without leaving a gap; the saved
  audio plays back seamlessly.
- **Stop** — ends the meeting and starts transcription.

The panel has three tabs, switchable with <kbd>⌘1</kbd>–<kbd>⌘3</kbd>:

- **Notes** (default) — type your own notes as the meeting runs. They're saved
  continuously. An **Insert** menu adds quick snippets like `/decision`,
  `/action`, and `/question`.
- **Transcript** — the live, speaker-labeled transcript. Toggle **Auto-scroll**
  or **Copy** the text so far.
- **Ask** — put a question to an AI provider about the conversation as it
  happens (see below).

### Hiding speakers

Sometimes you want to share a transcript without your own voice in it — or
without a noisy participant. In any saved meeting:

- **Hide my voice** — a one-click toggle at the top of the transcript removes
  your turns from the view. Nothing is destroyed; bring them back anytime with
  the same toggle.
- **Hide any speaker** — the **Speakers** tab (<kbd>⌘4</kbd> in the detail view)
  shows each participant's talk share. Click **Hide from transcript** on any
  speaker to remove their turns. Hidden speakers appear in a **Hidden** section
  with **Unhide** and **Unhide all** controls.

Hiding persists across re-analysis — if you re-run speaker detection later (to
pick up model improvements), the people you hid stay hidden.

## The floating pill

While a meeting is active, a small pill floats on top of every app. It pulses
with your audio levels while recording, dims with pause bars when paused, shows
a spinner while transcribing, and a checkmark when the meeting is saved.

- **Click** the pill to open the panel.
- **Right-click** for **Stop**, **Pause / Resume**, **Open**, and **Cancel**.
  Cancel discards the recording and deletes its audio, so it asks you to confirm
  first.

## Ask during a meeting

The **Ask** tab lets you query an AI provider about the live transcript and your
notes without leaving the call. The empty state offers browsable quick prompts
grouped under **Catch Up**, **Capture**, and **Challenge** — for example
"Summarize so far", "Action items", and "What should I ask?". Pinned follow-ups
like "Tell me more" and "Counter-argument?" sit above the input once a thread
is going.

Your Ask conversation carries over to the saved meeting, so you can keep working
with it afterward.

<Callout>
  Ask needs an AI provider; recording and transcription work without one.
  Connect a provider under **Settings → AI Provider** — a local provider keeps
  everything on your Mac.
</Callout>

## After a meeting

When you press **Stop**, Hindsight transcribes the audio on-device and saves the
meeting to your **Recent meetings** list. The pill confirms with a checkmark and
clears itself shortly after.

A saved meeting keeps its transcript, your notes, and any Ask conversation. You
can:

- **Rename** it from the title.
- Run **prompt templates** against it — Summary, Action Items & Decisions,
  Chapter Breakdown, and more.
- Continue the **chat** about it from the meeting's page.
- **Save Audio** to export the recording.

### Run a summary automatically

Turn on **Run marked prompts automatically** under "After each meeting" to have
chosen templates (the **Summary** template is marked by default) run as soon as
a meeting finishes. Because this sends the transcript to your AI provider, it's
off until you switch it on. Use **Edit Prompts…** to add, pin, hide, or reset
the built-in prompts.

## Privacy

Capture and transcription run entirely on your Mac. AI features — Ask, prompt
templates, and auto-summaries — only reach out when you've connected a provider,
and a local provider keeps everything on-device.
