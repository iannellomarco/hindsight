Hindsight is a private daybook for your Mac. It is one continuous book you turn
through, page by page: a **Day Journal** for what you worked on, **The Minutes**
for your meetings, and a **Second Brain** that learns from your history and can
talk it back to you.

The window is page-based. Turn left and right between days, jump anywhere with
the Index (<kbd>⌘K</kbd>), and let the red Ribbon bring you back to today.
Everything you capture stays on this Mac.

## The three pillars

### Day Journal

The Day Journal is today's page in your book — a running record of what you did,
so you can reflect on your decisions later. Turn between days with the left and
right arrows, and use **Ask the book** on any page to pose a question about your
day or your history.

[Read more about the Day Journal →](/docs/day-journal)

### The Minutes

The Minutes records and transcribes your meetings on-device. Start a recording
from the Index or with <kbd>⇧⌘M</kbd>; Hindsight can also watch your calendar
and offer to begin minutes when a meeting starts, recognizing Google Meet, Zoom,
and Microsoft Teams links. Finished meetings are saved to your Library with a
title you can rename.

[Read more about The Minutes →](/docs/meetings)

### Second Brain

The Second Brain learns from what you capture. Chat with your history, browse
the Insights it has noticed, explore the Network of how people, projects, and
ideas connect, and Export what you find — including drafting an email or a
calendar event from the conversation.

[Read more about the Second Brain →](/docs/second-brain)

## Privacy

Hindsight is on-device by default:

- **Capture, transcription, and storage stay local.** Meetings are transcribed
  on your Mac, and your journal database never leaves it.
- **No AI traffic without a provider you choose.** If you don't connect an AI
  provider, nothing is sent anywhere — Ask the book and the Second Brain simply
  wait until one is configured.
- **A local provider keeps everything on your machine.** Connect Ollama or
  LM Studio and your text is processed locally, with no calls off the Mac.
- **Cloud is opt-in.** Sending your journal to a cloud provider is off until you
  explicitly turn it on, and the same gate applies uniformly to journal text,
  meeting transcripts, chat, and drafts.
