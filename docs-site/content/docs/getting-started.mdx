## Requirements

- **macOS 14.2 or later.**
- **Apple Silicon** (M-series). The on-device speech model runs on the Neural
  Engine, so an Apple Silicon Mac is required.
- A few gigabytes of free disk space for the speech model, which downloads once
  during setup.

## Install

1. Download the latest `Hindsight.dmg` from the
   [Releases page](https://github.com/iannellomarco/hindsight/releases).
2. Open the DMG and drag **Hindsight** onto the **Applications** folder shortcut.
3. Open **Hindsight** from Applications.

Hindsight keeps itself up to date, and you can trigger a check yourself from the
Colophon under **The Apparatus → Check for updates**.

## First-time setup

The first time you open Hindsight, it opens a first-run setup that walks you
through a few short steps. A **Step _n_ of _N_** counter sits in the top-right,
and you can step back at any time with the chevron. Permission steps are
optional and can be skipped with **Skip — I'll set this up later**; only the
on-device model step has to finish before you continue.

### 1. Welcome

A quick tour of what Hindsight is: a **Day Journal** woven from periodic screen
capture, on-device meeting recording, and a design that keeps your data on this
Mac. Press **Get Started**.

### 2. Set up the Day Journal

The Day Journal periodically captures your screen and reads it with on-device
OCR to weave a private narrative of your day. Screenshots never leave your Mac.

This step asks for the **Screen & System Audio Recording** permission. Press
**Enable Day Journal**, then toggle Hindsight on in the System Settings pane
that opens. The same grant also covers meeting recording later, so you only
grant it once. See [Granting Screen Recording](#granting-screen-recording-on-recent-macos)
below if the status doesn't update right away.

The microphone is **not** required for journaling — you can skip the next step
and the Day Journal still works.

### 3. Microphone access (optional)

The microphone is only used for **meeting** capture. Grant it now if you plan to
record meetings, or skip it and enable it later from the Colophon.

### 4. Prepare on-device models

Hindsight downloads and optimizes the speech model on this Mac so meetings can
be transcribed locally — **audio never leaves your machine**. You'll see a
progress bar while the model downloads and warms up. This is the one step you
can't skip: **Continue** stays disabled until the model reports
**Speech model is ready.**

This download happens once. If a future launch already has the model cached, the
step finishes instantly. If setup stalls (for example, on a slow network), use
**Retry**.

### 5. Three moves, one rehearsal

Before you finish, Hindsight has you rehearse the three ways you move through
your book:

- **Turn** — the left and right arrows move between days.
- **The Index** — <kbd>⌘K</kbd> goes anywhere: days, minutes, settings.
- **The Ribbon** — the red mark always brings you back to today.

Press <kbd>⌘K</kbd> once when prompted to summon the Index, then press
<kbd>Return</kbd> to finish.

### 6. Ready

Press **Open Hindsight** and you're in. You'll land on today's page in your
Day Journal.

## Granting Screen Recording on recent macOS

The Day Journal and meeting recording both rely on the **Screen & System Audio
Recording** permission. On recent macOS, granting it has one quirk worth knowing.

1. In the setup step, press **Enable Day Journal** (or **Open System Settings**).
   This adds Hindsight to the list and opens
   **System Settings → Privacy & Security → Screen & System Audio Recording**.
2. **Toggle Hindsight on.** On recent macOS there's no confirmation dialog —
   flipping the toggle is the grant.
3. **Relaunch Hindsight.** macOS only applies this permission when the app
   starts, so the status may keep reading *Not granted* until you quit and
   reopen. Setup will prompt you to relaunch if it notices the grant hasn't
   taken effect yet.

If the Hindsight entry doesn't respond, remove it from the list and re-add
`/Applications/Hindsight.app` with the **+** button, then relaunch.

You can revisit any of this later. Screen Recording, microphone, and calendar
access can all be re-checked and changed from the **Colophon** (open the Index
with <kbd>⌘K</kbd> → **Colophon**).
